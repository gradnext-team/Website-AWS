# Case Structuring Multi-Select Questions - Quality Improvement Sample

## Summary
Improved 20 multi-select questions with high-quality distractor options following consulting best practices.

## Key Improvements

### 1. Consistent Option Level
**Before**: Mixed single concepts ("Revenue") with grouped concepts ("Fixed Costs, Variable Costs")  
**After**: All options at same abstraction level (all single concepts or all grouped)

### 2. Plausible Distractors
**Before**: Obviously wrong options that don't match format  
**After**: Realistic but MECE-violating options:
- Sub-components (wrong level)
- Overlapping categories (not mutually exclusive)
- Related but indirect factors

### 3. Increased Option Count
**Before**: 4-5 options  
**After**: 6-8 well-crafted options

## Sample Improved Questions

### Question 1: Profitability Analysis
**Correct**: Revenue, Costs  
**New Distractors**:
- Fixed Costs, Variable Costs (wrong level - sub-components)
- Pricing, Volume (wrong level - revenue drivers)
- Profit Margin (output, not input)
- Market Share (external metric)

### Question 4: Market Entry
**Correct**: Market Attractiveness, Competitive Landscape, Company Fit  
**New Distractors**:
- Market Size, Market Growth (sub-components of Attractiveness)
- Competitive Intensity (sub-component of Landscape)
- Internal Capabilities (sub-component of Fit)
- Customer Segments (implementation detail)

### Question 7: Hospital Wait Times  
**Correct**: Patient Arrival Process, Treatment Process, Discharge Process  
**New Distractors**:
- Registration, Triage, Consultation (sub-processes)
- Staffing Levels, Equipment Availability (resources, not stages)

### Question 8: SaaS Cost Structure
**Correct**: COGS, S&M, R&D, G&A  
**New Distractors**:
- Customer Success (overlaps with COGS or S&M)
- Engineering, Product Development (sub-components of R&D)
- Cloud Hosting (sub-component of COGS, too specific)

## Distractor Design Principles

1. **Wrong Level**: Sub-components or super-components of correct answers
2. **Overlap**: Options that violate mutual exclusivity
3. **Related but Indirect**: Factors that influence but aren't core components
4. **Implementation Detail**: Tactics vs. strategic structure
5. **Wrong Dimension**: Correct concepts but wrong categorization angle
